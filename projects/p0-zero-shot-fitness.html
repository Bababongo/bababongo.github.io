Summary

The result in one minute

P0 is a mechanism-sliced zero-shot protein fitness benchmark. It evaluates ESM-2, MSA conservation, and ProteinMPNN on public enzyme DMS datasets, then asks whether model performance changes near catalytic, metal-binding, ligand-contact, and active-site-neighborhood residues.

The result is not a simple foundation-model-wins story. ESM-2 gives useful global signal on multiple enzymes, but MSA conservation wins globally on AMIE and beta-glucosidase. ProteinMPNN is strongest overall on VIM-2, but weak at curated metal-site residues. Across the project, global mutation ranking and mechanism-local behavior can diverge.

P0 turns a vague concern, that protein models may fail near catalytic chemistry, into a concrete, reproducible, mechanism-sliced evaluation benchmark.

02 / The models

Three model signals, three biological priors

Each model family scores the same mutation differently because each encodes a different prior: sequence context, evolutionary family, or fixed-backbone structure.

ESM-2

sequence-context signal
score = log P(mutant aa at masked position) minus log P(wild-type aa at masked position)
Tests sequence-context plausibility learned from protein sequences.

MSA conservation

family-conservation signal
score = log frequency(mutant aa at aligned position) minus log frequency(wild-type aa at aligned position)
Tests whether the mutant is tolerated or disfavored across a protein family.

ProteinMPNN

fixed-backbone structural signal
score = log P(mutant aa | backbone) minus log P(wild-type aa | backbone)
Tests whether the mutant residue is compatible with a fixed backbone.
ProteinMPNN is not an enzyme activity model. It does not know the ligand, transition state, assay, or catalytic chemistry. It is a structure-conditioned baseline, and it is read here as one.

08 / Main scientific conclusion

Main scientific conclusion

Sequence-context, family-conservation, and fixed-backbone structure signals all help explain enzyme DMS fitness, but none of them remove the need for mechanism-sliced evaluation. A model can perform well globally while failing, weakening, or behaving ambiguously near catalytic, metal-binding, or active-site residues.

Evaluate protein models where the biology happens, not only across the whole sequence.

No single model family wins. The instrument is the mechanism-sliced comparison, not any one score.

09 / What P0 proves

What P0 proves

ESM-2 gives useful zero-shot signal

ESM-2 35M gives useful global signal on multiple enzyme DMS datasets.

Performance differs by residue class

Exact catalytic sites, metal sites, active-site neighborhoods, ligand contacts, and background residues can behave differently.

Exact catalytic analysis is fragile

Exact catalytic slices are often small and should not be overclaimed.

Neighborhoods can be informative

Broader active-site neighborhoods and ligand or metal shells can show stronger signal than exact catalytic residues alone.

Matched controls are required

Mechanism-slice lifts need null controls before becoming biological claims.

Classical baselines still matter

MSA conservation can beat ESM-2 on some enzymes.

Structure-conditioned scoring has limits

ProteinMPNN can be strong overall while weak at mechanism-local residues.

Comparison reveals inductive biases

ESM-2, MSA conservation, and ProteinMPNN each expose a different biological prior.

10 / The boundary of the claim

What P0 does not prove

  • ESM-2 understands catalysis.
  • ProteinMPNN understands enzyme activity.
  • Mechanism neighborhoods are always special.
  • Active-site residues always have lower model performance.
  • Retrospective DMS correlation is enough for enzyme design.
  • A single exact catalytic-site Spearman with tiny n is conclusive.

The value of P0 is not that it makes a universal claim. The value is that it prevents universal claims from being made too easily.

15 / Interview framing

How I explain P0

I built P0 to test whether protein language models fail differently near enzyme catalytic residues. Instead of only reporting whole-protein DMS correlation, I split variants by catalytic sites, metal sites, ligand contacts, catalytic shells, active-site neighborhoods, and background residues. Then I added matched controls so I could avoid overclaiming tiny residue groups. The benchmark compares ESM-2, MSA conservation, and ProteinMPNN, which separates sequence-context, evolutionary-family, and fixed-backbone structural signals. The most interesting final result is VIM-2: ProteinMPNN is best overall with Spearman 0.6259, but weak at curated metal-site residues with Spearman 0.2583 versus 0.6197 outside that group. That shows global mutation ranking can look strong while mechanism-local behavior remains a distinct failure mode.

I built a benchmark showing that protein models can rank enzyme mutations well overall while still behaving differently at catalytic or metal-site residues.

16 / Best next scientific step

Best next scientific step

The next scientific step is not to add models endlessly. The best next step is to design a prospective validation protocol where the P0 evaluation logic is used to choose variants before experimental or higher-fidelity computational validation.

If staying retrospective, the highest-value additions are focused:

1

Add a target-aligned TEM-1 ProteinMPNN profile.

2

Add one model-family summary table or figure for the website.

3

Add MSA Transformer only if it answers a specific unresolved question.

The model score is the beginning of the question, not the end of the experiment.
