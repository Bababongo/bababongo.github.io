Why this matters

Modern protein design does not fail only in the model. It fails in construct design, expression, assay sensitivity, product detection, substrate geometry, and interpretation.

My work focuses on the interface between computational design and experimental execution: turning protein-design hypotheses into testable variants and interpretable data.

01 / What I work on

Where protein design meets experimental execution.

I work at the interface between computational design and experimental execution: choosing which variants to build, designing constructs, running assays, measuring products, and learning from each failure.

01

AI-assisted engineering of Fdc1 / UbiD-family decarboxylases

Rational designHomologuesAI-assisted designprFMNGC-MS

Structure-guided and AI-assisted engineering of Fdc1 / UbiD-family decarboxylases for non-native cyclopropyl conjugated fatty acid analogue decarboxylation.

  • Diagnosing enzyme failure through substrate pose, active-site geometry, tunnel shape, and cofactor alignment.
  • Combining rational design, natural homologue screening, and ProteinMPNN / LigandMPNN-style variant prioritization.
  • Using GC-MS validation to connect design hypotheses to measurable product formation.
Current thesis direction · ongoing work

03

POP-FA production platform in Streptomyces

StreptomycesPOP-FAsPKS engineeringLC-MS

Engineering Streptomyces venezuelae as a production chassis for polycyclopropanated fatty acids and related fuel-like molecules.

  • Chromosomal integration of the pop biosynthetic gene cluster from Streptomyces roseoverticuli.
  • LC-MS confirmation of cyclopropanated fatty acid production.
  • Chimeric POP-PKS design and pathway engineering to diversify product structures.
  • Precursor-supply engineering through bauA and mcr-C constructs.
04

POP-FA to terminal alkene upgrading

OleTFdc1 / UbiDTerminal alkenesGC-MS

Developing enzyme-based routes to convert fatty acids and POP-FA analogues into terminal alkenes.

  • Purified-enzyme reactions with OleT-PRN on straight-chain fatty acid substrates.
  • GC-MS workflows for fatty acid and terminal alkene detection at low micromolar levels.
  • Current focus on decarboxylase engineering for more challenging cyclopropyl-containing substrates.

05 / About

About Eze

I'm Ezechinyere "Eze" Ukabiala, a UC Berkeley / UCSF Bioengineering PhD candidate in the Keasling Lab. My work connects protein engineering, enzyme redesign, metabolic engineering, analytical chemistry, and AI-assisted design workflows.

I'm especially interested in the step where computational protein-design hypotheses become real experiments: which variants to build, which controls to trust, which assays can see the product, and what failure teaches the next round.

My current scientific direction connects POP-FA microbial production, Fdc1 / UbiD-family enzyme engineering, and GC-MS / LC-MS-validated product upgrading.
